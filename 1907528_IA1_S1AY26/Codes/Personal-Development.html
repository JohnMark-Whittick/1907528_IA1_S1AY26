Book Reflections: The Twelve Pillars

Chapter 4: Achieve Your Goals

This chapter stood out to me because I struggle with direction and motivation. It showed me that writing goals down gives clarity and purpose, and that goals are valuable not just for what they achieve, but for who I become while working toward them.

Chapter 5: Proper Use of Time

Time management is one of my biggest challenges. This chapter made me realize that being busy is not the same as being productive. To grow, I need to prioritize important long-term goals instead of constantly reacting to short-term pressures.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself With the Best People

As a reserved person, building connections does not come naturally to me. This chapter challenged me to be intentional about who I surround myself with and to seek relationships that encourage growth, accountability, and positive influence.

Chapter 7: Be a Life-Long Learner

This chapter reminded me that continuous learning builds confidence and adaptability. Even when motivation is low, choosing learning over passive habits is essential for personal growth, especially in a fast-changing field like data analytics.

My SMART Goals

Advanced SQL Mastery

Specific: Complete a professional certification in Advanced SQL.

Measurable: Scoring 85% or higher on the certification exam.

Achievable: Using my foundation from UTech and Jamalco internship.

Relevant: Critical for high-level data analysis roles.

Time-bound: Completion by August 2026.

Professional Networking

Specific: Attend industry seminars to improve my social connection skills.

Measurable: Connect with at least 3 professionals per event.

Achievable: By stepping out of my comfort zone and preparing talking points.

Relevant: Overcoming my reserved nature to build a professional network.

Time-bound: Ongoing through the academic year.

Overall Reflection

Reflecting on these pillars has given me a clearer framework for addressing my struggles with motivation, time management, and building connections. By applying these principles gradually and intentionally, I am taking responsibility for shaping a more focused, balanced, and purposeful professional life.
