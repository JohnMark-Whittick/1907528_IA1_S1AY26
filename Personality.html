Favorite Idea: AI-Powered Predictive Maintenance

This idea immediately appealed to me because of my time interning at Jamalco, where I came into frequent contact with specialized instruments and heavy industrial equipment that require frequent maintenance. Applying AI-driven predictive maintenance in a world like Jamalco would involve using IoT sensors to monitor machine vibrations and temperatures in real-time. Instead of waiting for a breakdown, the AI would forecast failures before they happen. This would be incredibly useful as it prevents the massive costs of emergency repairs and the dangerous downtime that can halt production in a refinery. However, an ethical concern arises regarding Job Displacement; as AI takes over the monitoring role, the demand for traditional manual inspection roles may decrease. My solution is to implement "Technician Upskilling," where the existing workforce is trained to manage and interpret the AI data rather than being replaced by it. An actual application of this technology is seen in GE Digital’s SmartSignal, which helps industrial plants predict and eliminate equipment failure.
